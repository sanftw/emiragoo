A Foodie’s Guide to Traditional Emirati Cuisine

The UAE is known for its futuristic skyline and luxury experiences, but nestled within its modern exterior is a rich culinary heritage waiting to be explored. Traditional Emirati cuisine tells the story of the region’s culture, values, and way of life—built on hospitality, simplicity, and bold flavors.


Start with the Staples

A great introduction to Emirati food begins with Harees, a comforting dish made with wheat and slow-cooked meat. Another staple is Machboos, a spiced rice dish infused with dried lemon (loomi), saffron, and an aromatic blend of spices—usually paired with chicken or lamb. Both dishes reflect the Emiratis' love for slow-cooked, hearty meals.


Breakfast the Local Way

Begin your morning with Balaleet—a delightful contrast of sweet saffron vermicelli noodles topped with a savory egg omelet. Often served with karak tea, it's a must-try for those who enjoy unique flavor pairings.


Don’t Miss the Street Snacks

Traditional snacks like Luqaimat, deep-fried dough balls drizzled with date syrup, are a favorite during Ramadan and festive occasions. These bite-sized treats are crispy on the outside and soft inside, perfect for a quick indulgence.


Flavors from the Sea

Given the UAE’s strong coastal heritage, seafood plays a central role. Dishes such as Jesheed (baby shark with rice and spices) reflect the nation's connection to the sea and its historic pearl-diving roots.


Savor the Culture

Beyond just taste, Emirati cuisine emphasizes sharing, gathering, and generosity. Most meals are served in large portions, meant to be enjoyed together, reflecting the deep-rooted value of community.


Exploring Emirati cuisine is more than just a food journey—it's a cultural experience. Whether you're visiting a traditional restaurant or being welcomed into a local home, these dishes offer a delicious glimpse into the heart of the UAE.
